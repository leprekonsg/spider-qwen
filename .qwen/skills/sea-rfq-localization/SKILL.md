# SEA RFQ Localization

Use when drafting or evaluating supplier-facing RFQ language for Southeast Asia.

Guidelines:
- Keep tone direct, polite, and procurement-neutral.
- Prefer concise English that works across Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines.
- Ask for scope, pricing basis, service frequency, lead time, contract terms, and minimum order or contract conditions.
- Do not imply the RFQ has been sent or submitted.
- Preserve evidence caveats: if a quote channel, service scope, or price is missing, say that human review is required.
